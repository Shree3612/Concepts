
# Listening and Active Communication

## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)

- Pay full attention to the speaker.
- Show that you are listening through body language and gestures.
- Provide feedback by summarizing or paraphrasing.
- Defer judgment and avoid interrupting.
- Respond appropriately and ask questions if needed.
- Remember key points and emotions conveyed.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? 

- Mirror what the speaker says and feels.
- Show understanding of their emotions and message.
- Confirm what was understood by reflecting back.
- Build trust and reduce tension in conversations.
- Helps in coming to agreements and clarifying ideas.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- Distractions or multitasking.
- Preconceived judgments or biases.
- Emotional reactions blocking understanding.
- Interrupting the speaker.
- Lack of interest or motivation to listen.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

- Focus fully on the speaker without distractions.
- Keep patience and avoid interrupting.
- Ask open-ended questions to clarify.
- Take notes to remember details.
- Keep an open mind and avoid assumptions.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?

- When avoiding conflict to keep peace.
- When feeling insecure or afraid to speak up.
- When wanting to please others even at own cost.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?

- When feeling frustrated or angry.
- When trying to control or dominate a situation.
- When defending strongly against the threats.

## 7.  When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?

- When unwilling to express feelings openly.
- Using sarcasm or silent treatment to show displeasure.
- When indirect expression of anger or frustration is preferred.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyse the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life?

- Clearly express own thoughts and feelings respectfully.
- Maintain calm and steady body language.
- Use "I" statements to take ownership of feelings.
- Listen actively and respond thoughtfully.
- Set boundaries without being aggressive or passive.
# Listening and Active Communication

## 1. What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)

- Give your full attention to the person speaking.  
- Show you are listening by nodding or using your face and body.  
- Repeat or say in your own words what they said to make sure you understand.  
- Don’t interrupt or judge while they are talking.  
- Ask questions if you don’t understand.  
- Remember important points and feelings they share.

## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

- Copy what the person says and how they feel.  
- Show you understand their feelings and message.  
- Repeat back to them what you understood to check if it's right.  
- Build trust and help calm tense talks.  
- Help both sides agree and understand each other better.

## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- Getting distracted or doing other things while listening.  
- Having an opinion before hearing the whole story.  
- Letting emotions like anger stop you from listening well.  
- Interrupting the speaker.  
- Not feeling interested or motivated to listen.

## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

- Focus only on the speaker, no distractions.  
- Be patient and don’t interrupt.  
- Ask questions to make things clearer.  
- Write down important details to not forget.  
- Keep an open mind and don’t guess what they mean.

## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?

- When you want to avoid fights or keep peace.  
- When you feel shy or scared to say what you think.  
- When you want to make others happy even if it’s at your own cost.

## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?

- When you feel angry or upset.  
- When you want to control or be the boss in situation.  
- When you feel threatened and want to defend yourself strongly.

## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?

- When you don’t want to say your feelings openly.  
- When you use sarcasm or ignore someone to show you are unhappy.  
- When you express anger or frustration in an indirect way.

## 8. How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyse the videos, then think what would be a few steps you can apply in your own life?

- Say what you think and feel clearly but kindly.  
- Stay calm and use steady body language.  
- Use “I” sentences like “I feel...” to own your feelings.  
- Listen well and answer carefully.  
- Say no or set limits without being rude or quiet.
